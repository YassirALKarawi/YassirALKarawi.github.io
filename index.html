Research perspective

From physical links to certified network intelligence.

My research examines how future communication systems can become more energy-aware, secure, interoperable, and trustworthy. The work spans physical-layer and optical transport problems through to Open RAN control, quantum-enabled networking, and AI-enabled network architectures.

View research themes

Research themes

A connected research programme

Five technical directions linked by a common focus on measurable performance, security, interoperability, and reproducibility.

01

Open RAN & 6G control

Energy-aware optimisation, virtualised RAN architectures, near-real-time control, and efficient resource allocation.

02

Quantum communications

Entanglement quality, QKD-assisted security, quantum-aware networking, and thermal-loss channel modelling.

03

AI-native digital twins

Digital-twin architectures, native-AI network management, secure learning, and evaluation frameworks.

04

Wireless & spectrum systems

5G/6G mobile networks, cognitive radio, propagation, OFDM, HAPS coexistence, and spectrum-sharing optimisation.

05

Optical networks & signal processing

DWDM transport, optical-network monitoring, digital-filter design, FPGA architectures, and communication backhaul.
